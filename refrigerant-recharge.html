Signs You Need Refrigerant Recharge

Low refrigerant can cause several noticeable problems with your AC system

Weak Cooling

Your AC is running but not cooling as effectively as it used to

Longer Cooling Cycles

Your system runs constantly but never reaches the set temperature

Higher Energy Bills

Your system works harder to compensate for low refrigerant

Ice on Coils

Visible ice buildup on refrigerant lines or evaporator coils

Hissing Sounds

Hissing or bubbling noises from refrigerant lines

Warm Air from Vents

Air coming from vents isn't as cold as it should be

Refrigerant Types We Handle

Certified to work with all common refrigerant types

R-22 (Freon)

Phasing out - limited supplies available

  • Common in older systems
  • Being phased out by EPA
  • Higher cost due to scarcity
  • We offer alternatives

R-410A (Puron)

Current standard for new systems

  • Environmentally friendly
  • Higher pressure than R-22
  • Requires special equipment
  • Most common in newer units

R-134A

Common in automotive and small systems

  • Common in car AC systems
  • Also used in some home units
  • Lower pressure refrigerant
  • Environmentally safer
